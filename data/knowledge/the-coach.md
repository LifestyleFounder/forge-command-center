## THE 10.0 PROCESS OVERVIEW

### Three Ingredients for Transformation:
1. **Vision** — A big, inspiring, clear picture that pulls you forward
2. **Belief** — Belief in your ability to achieve your goals
3. **Time** — Spending focused time thinking about, dreaming about, and becoming that vision

### The Gap (Why You're Not There Yet):
The only reason someone isn't at their 10.0 is they're missing one or more of:
- **Character traits**
- **Beliefs**
- **Skills**

### Tony Robbins' Three Elements of Mastery:
1. **Modeling** — Find someone ahead of you and study them
2. **Total Immersion** — Be fully in the environment
3. **Spaced Repetition** — Repeat your goals, vision, and affirmations daily

### Language → See → Action → Results
How we speak our world informs how we see our world, which creates our actions and the results we create.

### Standards vs Goals (Sam Ovens):
"We are not the highest version of ourselves we can imagine. We are the lowest version of ourselves we can accept."

### The Perfect System (Rich Litvin):
Take an area where you're stuck. Design the "perfect system" for getting that exact stuck result. The awareness alone shifts behavior.

## STEP 1: R.E.S.P.E.C.T. VISION

Guide users through 7 life areas, ONE at a time:

1. **R — Relationships & Romance** — Who do you want to do life with? Think about all relationships including romantic partner.
2. **E — Exercise & Eating** — Changes to diet and exercise. How do you want to look? Feel? Eat? Why?
3. **S — Spirituality & Self-Expression** — Areas of self to expand. Spiritual practice development.
4. **P — Passion & Purpose** — Business, work, calling. Find the intersection of: passionate about, aligned with purpose, solves a real problem, people will pay for it, you're actually good at it.
5. **E — Education & Experiences** — What to learn? What experiences to have?
6. **C — Contribution & Charity** — What's the contribution? Charitable giving? "We don't make a life by what we get, but what we give."
7. **T — Time, Travel & Things** — Distinguish between means goals (stepping stones) and ends goals (the point).

After EACH response, write a vivid, emotionally inspiring paragraph in present tense.

## STEP 2: BELIEF REWIRING (Byron Katie's The Work)

### Part One: The Perfect System Exercise
Take an area of life where you're experiencing resistance. Map out how you're creating it. Example: "The perfect system for a Constant Roller Coaster ride of income" — Don't do any prospecting, Only do launches once or twice a year, Don't ask for referrals, etc.

### Part Two: Uncovering Limiting Beliefs

**Step 1:** Write down all things you've repeatedly tried to do/get/accomplish where you've come up short. For each, list a reason why.

**Step 2:** Get still and listen. Write down negative thinking you notice that you really believe to be true.

**Step 3:** Write out thoughts you know aren't fully true but pop up anyway.

**Step 4:** Common limiting beliefs checklist:
- "I'm not attractive"
- "The world is dangerous"
- "Rich people are greedy and selfish"
- "I'll never succeed"
- "Nobody takes me seriously"
- "I'm not talented"
- "It's too good, I don't deserve it"
- "I'm too lazy"
- "I have no discipline"
- "I'm not enough"
- "I'm not good enough"

**Step 5:** Find top 5 negative thoughts/beliefs. Identify the deepest core issue.

**Step 6:** Journal freely about what you've discovered.

### Part Three: Rewiring Limiting Beliefs

Three rules: Be open-minded. Work to find new perspectives. Don't take this too seriously.

For each limiting belief, ask:
1. "Is it true?"
2. "Can you absolutely know that it's true?"
3. "How do you react, what happens, when you believe that thought?"
4. "Who would you be without it?"

Then create a new choice: "I am stupid" becomes → "My new choice is: I am a wise and intelligent soul"

### Reinforcing with Declarations
Write out, repeat out loud, post them around your house, carry them in your pockets, create alarms on your phone. Make a voice recording and marinate your mind with these affirmations day and night for 31 days.

T. Harv Eker technique: Touch your head or heart as you repeat declarations out loud.

Write 50-100 positive affirmations aligned with your new inner image.

## ENGINEERING YOUR PERFECT WEEK

### Things to Eliminate:
1. **Buffer Time** — Audit what you do in 15-30 min blocks. Find the scroll gaps.
2. **Bleed Time** — Meetings shouldn't bleed into each other. Honor boundaries.
3. **$10 Tasks** — Anything a VA could do, delegate immediately.

### Things to Optimize:
1. **Optimize for Energy** — Not just time. Morning person? Creative work first. Calls in afternoon.
2. **Find NET Time (No Extra Time)** — Combine monotonous tasks with learning (driving + podcast, bike + DMs).
3. **Task Batching** — Group similar work into blocks. Coaching blocks, content blocks, sales blocks.

### Key Principles:
- Be willing to iterate — seasons change
- Honor your Perfect Week — time is your most valuable asset
- It's about energy, not just time
- Important goes first (family, revenue-driving work)
- Then put in everything else
- Batch work for flow
- The calendar always precedes the cash flow
- Create white space for new creation
- Move from reactive → proactive
